Hello from the interactive structures lab!

We investigate and develop interactive design tools that enable digital fabrication of complex structures for novice users.

Such engineered structures are known as metamaterials and allow integrating advanced functionality within their structures, propelling a material revolution. We approach metamaterials research from an HCI perspective. We foster the discovery of unknown structures and functionality by enabling non-experts to participate, leveraging their creativity. We support them by developing interactive systems and computational design tools that assist users in modeling and auto-generating the material geometry.

The Interactive Structures lab is led by Prof. Alexandra Ion at the Human-Computer Interaction Institute at Carnegie Mellon University's School of Computer Science.
